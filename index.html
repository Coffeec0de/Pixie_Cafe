    Manifacturing Steps

    01.

    Harvesting of coffee cherries typically takes place between seven to nine months after the plant has bloomed, depending on the variety and climate. Each coffee cherry typically contains two seeds, known as beans, which are positioned against one anther.

    02.

    The dry process of coffee production involves sorting and cleaning fruits, then placing them in the sun to dry on patios or mats. This process can take up to four weeks, during which time the cherries are raked or turned to prevent fermentation and mold.

    03.

    Roasting is a vital step in producing coffee beans for use in the home, and involves transforming green beans to their aromatic brown counterparts. Roasting is usually done at temperatures of around 550F, with the beans turning continuously to prevent them from burning.
